## 1. App Overview & Objectives

**Objective:**
Build a highly usable, impact-focused internal dashboard for municipal corporations to **measure, improve, and act on road-dust mitigation through water sprinkling**. The system prioritizes **before-vs-after impact**, actionable insights, and simple UI over public reporting or AI showmanship.

**What this is not:**

* Not a citizen-facing or PR dashboard
* Not a futuristic or experimental system
* Not a complex analytics platform

**What this is:**

* An operational control panel for ward-level engineers
* A measurement + advisory system to improve AQI outcomes
* A defensible, immediately deployable solution

## 4. Core Features & Functionality

### 4.1 Sensor-Based Measurement

* Fixed air-quality sensor units placed along:

  * Dust-prone roads
  * Construction-heavy wards
  * Sprinkler routes (before & after)
* Sensors include:

  * PM2.5 / PM10
  * Humidity (mandatory)
  * Temperature (optional)

### 4.2 Impact Measurement Logic (Locked)

* **Simplest operational model**:

  * Pre-sprinkling PM reading
  * Post-sprinkling PM reading after a fixed time window
* Focuses on **impact**, not absolute AQI compliance

### 4.3 Advisory (Not Directive) Recommendations

* System suggests (engineer decides):

  * Which routes matter most today
  * Best time to sprinkle for maximum impact
* Recommendations are **advisory-only** to ensure adoption and trust

### 4.4 Daily Structured Action Plans

* Three precise plans generated per day:

  * Morning
  * Evening
  * Night
* Each plan highlights:

  * ğŸš¨ Routes requiring sprinkling (high impact expected)
  * Routes where sprinkling is optional or low ROI

### 4.5 Contractor Performance Monitoring

* Tracks repeated ineffective sprinkling
* Flags skipped high-impact routes
* Generates contractor performance alerts for supervisors

## 6. Decision & Update Model

### 6.1 Data Refresh

* **Near-real-time updates** (every few minutes)
* Supports live monitoring

## 7. Alerting Strategy (Strict & Limited)

Alerts triggered only when:

1. Repeated ineffective sprinkling on the same route
2. High-impact route skipped
3. AQI worsening despite sprinkling

> Fewer alerts = higher trust
